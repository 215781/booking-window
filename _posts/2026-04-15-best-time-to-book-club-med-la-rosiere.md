Club Med La Rosière sits in an unusual position in the French Alps portfolio. At 1,850m above the Tarentaise valley — with a direct ski connection into Italy via the Mont Blanc ski area — it runs a longer season than almost every other Club Med resort and opens one of the earliest December departure dates in the range. Price data tracked from April 2026 shows a spread from **£3,288 to £9,716** for a 7-night, 2-adult package across the 2026/27 season. *Prices updated 22 June 2026.*

Understanding that range — and specifically when prices sit at either end of it — is the purpose of this article.

## The extended season is La Rosière's defining data point

Most Club Med French Alps resorts open for Sunday departures from late December onwards. La Rosière starts on **13 December** — roughly two weeks ahead of the typical portfolio opening — and runs through to **11 April**, giving it one of the longest observable windows in the range.

Those bookend weeks are also where the most competitive prices sit. The 13 December departure (£3,618–£4,180 for 2 adults) and the 11 April departure (£3,288–£3,798) represent the season's floor — and both are weeks that many other Club Med resorts simply do not offer.

For families who can travel outside peak school holiday dates, this is materially valuable. December 13 falls before both French and UK school Christmas holidays begin; April 11 falls at or just after the Easter school break in most regions. Both weeks offer a full-season ski experience at La Rosière's altitude with considerably less resort traffic.

## How the half-term premium compares

La Rosière's February pricing is notable for what it *doesn't* do. Across the portfolio, February half-term triggers significant spikes: at Val d'Isère, half-term reaches £13,244 for 2 adults. At La Rosière, the same half-term period shows prices of £6,220–£6,294 — elevated versus January, but not the dramatic premium seen at the prestige resorts.

One interpretation: La Rosière's longer season distributes demand more evenly. The resort has weeks available before and after the main school holiday windows, which gives it a different supply-demand profile than resorts constrained to a tighter operating window. It also means the urgency of booking well in advance for half-term is somewhat lower here than at more constrained alternatives.

## A note on departure day pricing at La Rosière

The tracked data for La Rosière includes both Saturday and Sunday departure dates. Readers comparing prices should note that Saturday departures at this resort show meaningfully higher prices than adjacent Sunday departures in some weeks. For mid-January, Saturday departures tracked as high as £9,716, while the equivalent Sunday departures in January are now priced at £4,618.

If you are comparing La Rosière prices across booking platforms, verify that you are comparing equivalent departure days. The Sunday departure price — which is the standard for most Club Med French Alps resorts — is the more representative benchmark for cross-resort comparisons.

## The best weeks for value at La Rosière

Three windows stand out in the current data:

**11 April (£3,288 – £3,798 for 2 adults):** The cheapest week in the season, made possible by the resort's unusually late closing date. April skiing in the Mont Blanc area tends to deliver spring conditions at their most enjoyable — long daylight, soft afternoon snow, reliable cover at La Rosière's elevation. This week is inaccessible at most other Club Med resorts.

**13 December (£3,618 – £4,180 for 2 adults):** The early opening week delivers Christmas-adjacent atmosphere without Christmas-week pricing. Conditions in early December at 1,850m are typically reliable once the season properly opens. For families with flexibility around school terms or professional adults without school-calendar constraints, this week consistently represents the best-value entry point into the La Rosière season.

**March shoulder (£3,740 – £4,902 for 2 adults):** From mid-March onwards, prices drop materially. March at La Rosière's elevation typically offers excellent snow and increasingly good spring weather. These weeks sit at roughly 60–70% of the February half-term peak.

## When to book

La Rosière's longer season and less concentrated half-term spike mean there is generally less urgency around booking timing than at resorts where the peak periods represent a larger share of total availability. The key exception is the December 13 departure: as the earliest opening week, it has limited availability and no comparable earlier alternative. If that date is your target, early booking is sensible.

## La Rosière's position in the portfolio

For the week of 3 January 2027, La Rosière is priced at £4,618 for 2 adults — above Valmorel (£4,272–£4,696) and comparable to Les Arcs (approximately £5,396–£5,934), and well below Val d'Isère (£12,026). The Mont Blanc ski connection — which gives access to Courmayeur on the Italian side — adds a point of differentiation against similarly priced alternatives in the portfolio.

The [Club Med Tignes vs Les Arcs](/blog/club-med-tignes-vs-les-arcs/) article provides a useful cross-resort comparison at a similar price tier.

## Practical takeaway

- **Flexible on dates?** Early December (13 Dec) and late April (11 Apr) are the value endpoints — both unique to La Rosière's extended season.
- **Committed to February?** Half-term pricing here is elevated but notably less spiked than at Valmorel, Val d'Isère or Tignes. La Rosière is among the more manageable half-term options in the portfolio.
- **Comparing departure days?** Check Sunday vs Saturday prices carefully — the tracked data shows a significant price difference between the two at La Rosière in certain weeks.
