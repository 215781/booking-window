<h1>Our Story</h1>
<p class="standfirst">How a family ski trip — and one overheard conversation — became a price intelligence tool.</p>

Skiing is everything to me. I try to go every year, and I could not wait for my daughter to turn four so we could take her for her first proper trip. But planning a ski holiday with a young child is a different beast entirely. How close is the accommodation to ski school? Will there be food she'll actually eat? And — honestly — how much is all of this going to cost?

My partner cut through the noise: just book Club Med. And boy, was she right. We went to Club Med La Plagne and had the absolute time of our lives. It couldn't have been more perfect.

When we got home, I kept thinking about a conversation I'd had with another guest on that trip.

<div class="stat-callout">
  <p>They had enjoyed the exact same resort, the exact same week — for £1,600 less than we paid.</p>
  <span>Not because they'd found a discount. Because of <em>when</em> they'd booked.</span>
</div>

Club Med was worth every penny — it genuinely is a priceless experience. But that conversation stuck with me. Wouldn't it be useful to know, before you commit, whether you're booking at the right moment? Which weeks tend to sell out early and go up in price as they do? Which weeks are quieter, and might drop in price if a cancellation comes in?

That question became WhenToBook.

This is not a discount site. There are no voucher codes here, no cashback schemes. It's a timing tool — somewhere you can find out whether to get in there quick because a departure date is selling out fast, or whether to hold on because prices for a particular week have just softened.

<div class="what-we-do">
  <h2>What the site does today</h2>
  <p>WhenToBook tracks prices daily across a growing range of ski holiday operators and resorts. For each departure date it monitors, it shows you whether pricing is trending up, trending down, or holding steady — so you can make a properly informed decision about when to book, rather than just guessing.</p>
</div>

The ambition is simple: to give every family the intelligence they need to be the cheaper family — not by cutting corners on the holiday, but by getting the timing right.
