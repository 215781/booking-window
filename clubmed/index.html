Signal Guide

What each signal means

Favourable

"Conditions suggest a reasonable time to book."

Price is stable or has plateaued at a historically favourable level, or availability signals suggest further drops are unlikely. The data points toward acting now — but timing is yours to judge.

Watch

"Price has moved recently. Direction is still resolving."

Price has shifted meaningfully in the past week, or the booking window suggests movement is likely soon. The picture isn't clear enough to signal either way — check back in the next few days.

Hold

"Stable conditions. Further drops are likely."

Price has risen recently, availability is comfortable, and the booking window suggests further drops are likely before the season closes. Patience is the rational position here.

The Method

How When To Book works

01

We track live Club Med pricing, updated daily.

Every major ski resort. Every Saturday departure across the season. Standard party sizes. We've been building this price history since spring 2026 — and it compounds in value with every passing week.

02

We analyse trends and availability over time.

A raw price is just a number. Context is what makes it actionable. We measure price direction, velocity of change, and availability tightening — the signals that Club Med's own site cannot give you.

03

We tell you whether to book now, watch, or hold.

No noise. No raw data dumps. A clear three-state signal per combination, updated daily. The decision is always yours — we just make sure you're making it with the right information.
